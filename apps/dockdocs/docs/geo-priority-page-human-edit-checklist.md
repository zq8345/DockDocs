# DockDocs GEO Priority Page Human Edit Checklist

This checklist is for human content review of the 20 priority GEO pages. Codex may prepare structure and validation, but a human editor should decide whether a page needs real examples, clearer boundaries, stronger comparison evidence, or less template-like wording.

## Review principles

- Do not invent customer stories, legal examples, financial facts, OCR accuracy rates, or platform limits.
- Keep every recommendation tied to visible DockDocs workflow content.
- Prefer concrete workflow boundaries over broad marketing claims.
- Remove repeated sentence patterns when two priority pages sound too similar.
- Keep AI as an assistive workflow layer, not a final authority for legal, financial, medical, academic, or compliance decisions.

## Human edit checklist fields

Use these checks for every priority page:

- Target queries present and specific.
- Quick answer is direct and extractable.
- AI answer snippet is 40-70 words and not promotional.
- Citation-ready facts are short, factual, and non-exaggerated.
- Workflow steps match the visible page topic.
- Limitations are specific to the workflow.
- Privacy notes match file sensitivity.
- Comparison evidence is added where the page compares workflows.
- File limit notes are present where upload or email size is part of the query.
- Local, device, or platform nuance is present where relevant.
- Template-like sentences are removed or rewritten.
- Internal links point to related tools and guides.

## Per-page human edit checklist

For each page, the editor should answer:

1. Does the first screen answer the target query without requiring a user to read the entire page?
2. Are the limitations specific enough for this page, or could they apply to any PDF workflow?
3. Does the page include at least one useful alternative workflow?
4. Are sensitive contexts handled carefully without legal, medical, financial, academic, or compliance overclaims?
5. Are the internal links clearly related to the user intent?
6. Are repeated phrases from other priority pages rewritten?
7. Does the page explain what a user should verify after exporting or using AI?
8. Does the page avoid exact file size, OCR accuracy, or platform-limit claims unless source-verified?
9. Is the final recommendation actionable without sounding promotional?

## Recommended review order

1. Compression and email pages.
2. OCR and scanned PDF pages.
3. AI PDF limitation and contract review pages.
4. Comparison pages.
5. Industry pages.

## Manual action required

Human editors should decide whether to add real examples only when examples can be written as generic workflows or verified business scenarios. Do not fabricate customer names, case studies, legal outcomes, OCR accuracy percentages, or email provider limits.
